Top buyer questions

These are the ornamental fence questions serious buyers usually ask before placing the first order.

Strong ornamental fence pages do more than show styles. They explain material choice, application routing, gate planning, and export logistics in the same place.

Will aluminum ornamental fence rust?

No. Aluminum does not rust the way steel does. Aluminum forms a natural oxide layer that protects the base metal from further corrosion. In pool and coastal environments, this natural protection is enhanced by the powder coat finish, making aluminum ornamental fence one of the most corrosion-resistant fence options available. For coastal projects within 1–5 km of saltwater, aluminum ornamental with powder coat is the preferred recommendation over steel.

Will steel ornamental fence rust over time?

Steel ornamental fence can rust if the coating is damaged or if it is used in a harsh coastal environment without adequate protection. The standard protection route is hot-dip galvanising (zinc coating) plus a powder coat finish on top — called duplex coating. This gives two layers of protection. In moderate inland environments, powder-coated steel ornamental fence typically lasts 15–25 years with minimal maintenance. In coastal environments, aluminum or duplex-coated steel is recommended.

Aluminum vs. steel ornamental fence — which should I choose?

Choose aluminum if the project is in a coastal or pool environment, or if lightweight handling and installation are important. Choose steel if the project needs higher physical strength, a heavier visual perimeter for security visibility, or a tighter budget for larger commercial quantities. Both can be powder coated in the same RAL color range and have comparable lifespans when properly maintained. The decision usually comes down to: coastal/pool = aluminum; inland commercial or budget-sensitive = steel.

What RAL colors are available for ornamental fence?

Standard RAL colors quoted from stock include RAL 6005 (forest green), RAL 7016 (anthracite grey), RAL 9005 (jet black), and RAL 9010 (pure white). Custom RAL colors are available with a small tooling or setup cost and typically add 5–10 working days to the production lead time. Color chips and sample panels can be provided before committing to a custom color run.

What heights are available for ornamental fence?

Standard ornamental fence heights typically range from 600 mm to 2400 mm. The most common residential heights are 900 mm, 1200 mm, and 1500 mm. Commercial and security applications commonly use 1800 mm and 2100 mm. Custom heights outside this range can be discussed from the first inquiry, particularly for pool compliance or perimeter security applications with specific height requirements.

Are ornamental fence panels suitable for pool compliance requirements?

Yes. Aluminum ornamental fence is commonly used for pool enclosures where local pool safety codes apply. Key configuration points: panel height must typically meet local minimums (often 1200 mm from finished floor level), picket spacing must prevent child access (typically no gap greater than 100 mm at the bottom 900 mm), and the gate must be self-closing and self-latching. We can configure height, picket spacing, and gate hardware to match common compliance requirements. Confirm the specific standard or code with your inquiry so we can verify the specification before quoting.

What gate types are available with ornamental fence?

The standard gate types include: pedestrian single-leaf gate (typically 900–1200 mm wide), vehicle double-leaf gate (typically 3000–4800 mm wide per leaf), cantilever or track sliding gate for driveways and plant access, and automation-compatible gate frames for motorisation. Gate hardware — hinges, latch, lock, and drop bolt — is included as standard or specified at the inquiry stage. Automation hardware and access control systems can be discussed as part of the project scope.

Is there a minimum order quantity for ornamental fence?

No MOQ for sample orders. Standard ornamental fence panel and gate orders typically have a 50–100 panel MOQ for production runs. Sample orders of 1–10 panels are available so you can validate finish quality, profile match, and color before committing to a full order. Sample costs and shipping are quoted separately and typically credited against a subsequent full order.

How long does it take to get ornamental fence samples?

Sample panels — typically 1–2 panels plus one post — can be prepared in 3–5 working days and shipped by express courier for faster delivery. Sample gate sets may take 5–10 working days depending on complexity. Sample lead time is separate from the production lead time for the full order and is confirmed in the sample inquiry response.

What does export packing look like for ornamental fence?

Ornamental fence panels are packed in bundles with cardboard interleaving between panels to protect the powder coat finish during transit. Posts are packed separately in bundles with protective wrapping. Gates are typically packed on pallets with foam or cardboard corner protection and stretch wrapping. All components are labeled by SKU and packed to minimise damage risk in 20ft or 40ft containers. Loading plans and packing diagrams are shared before dispatch so your team can plan receiving and offloading.

Material comparison

Route the buyer by project environment, strength requirement, and budget — not by one ornamental fence phrase.

Ornamental fence buyers often compare aluminum and steel before committing. This comparison is structured by the three factors that matter most in the decision: corrosion environment, strength, and budget.

Aluminum Ornamental Fence

Best for coastal environments, pool enclosures, residential properties, and projects where lightweight handling and maximum corrosion resistance are the primary drivers.

  • Natural oxide layer + powder coat = no rust in coastal and pool environments
  • Lightweight — easier handling, lower freight weight per panel
  • Standard heights 600–2400 mm; custom heights available
  • RAL powder coat from stock or custom colour matching
  • Gate options: pedestrian, vehicle, cantilever sliding, automation-compatible

Steel Ornamental Fence

Best for inland commercial, industrial, civic, and high-security applications where strength, visual deterrence, and budget compatibility are the primary drivers.

  • Hot-dip galvanised + powder coat (duplex) = two-layer protection
  • Higher physical strength and visual security presence
  • Standard heights 900–2400 mm; commercial heights 1800–2400 mm common
  • RAL powder coat from stock or custom colour matching
  • Gate options: pedestrian, vehicle double-leaf, cantilever, automation-compatible

Application routing

Ornamental fence buyers usually start from one of four application routes — not from one generic product keyword.

Matching the ornamental fence to the application first means the material, height, gate, and finish are all configured correctly before the first quote.

Residential

Single-family homes, gated communities, and residential estates where appearance, maintenance load, and colour consistency matter most.

  • Aluminum ornamental — preferred for most residential settings
  • Heights typically 900–1500 mm
  • Pedestrian gate + vehicle gate standard
  • RAL colour to match house trim or landscaping

Pool Enclosure

Pool safety fences and pool perimeter fences that need to meet local pool safety codes while maintaining visual appeal.

  • Aluminum ornamental — preferred for all pool environments
  • Height and picket spacing configured to local pool code
  • Self-closing, self-latching gate hardware included
  • Corrosion-proof hardware for pool chemical exposure

Coastal & Marine

Seaside residential, coastal commercial, and marine facility perimeters where salt-spray corrosion is the primary design constraint.

  • Aluminum ornamental — mandatory for direct salt exposure
  • Heavy powder coat thickness recommended (80–120 microns)
  • Stainless steel or hot-dip galvanised gate hardware for coastal
  • Marine-grade accessories and fixings available

Commercial & Civic

Office parks, schools, civic buildings, hotels, and public facilities where appearance, durability, and maintenance cost are all weighted.

  • Steel ornamental or aluminum ornamental depending on environment
  • Heights typically 1500–2100 mm
  • Larger vehicle gates, mixed gate configurations
  • RAL colour matched to building architecture or municipal standard

Finish, colour & quality

Ornamental fence finish choices affect long-term appearance, maintenance cost, and corrosion resistance — not just aesthetics.

The finish route is one of the most commonly under-specified items in ornamental fence RFQs. This section explains the options so buyers can include the right finish requirement in the first inquiry.

Powder coat finish (aluminum)

Thermosetting powder applied electrostatically and cured under heat. Standard thickness 60–80 microns. Available in all RAL colours. Provides a hard, smooth surface that is resistant to chipping, scratching, and UV fading. Recommended for residential, pool, and coastal applications.

Duplex coating — hot-dip galvanised + powder coat (steel)

Hot-dip galvanising applies a zinc layer to the steel (typically 45–85 microns depending on steel thickness). Powder coat is applied on top after pre-treatment. The combined system provides cathodic protection (galvanising) plus physical barrier (powder coat). Recommended for steel ornamental in inland and moderate coastal environments.

RAL colour matching

Standard RAL colours from stock: RAL 6005 (forest green), RAL 7016 (anthracite grey), RAL 9005 (jet black), RAL 9010 (pure white). Custom RAL matching is available with a small setup cost and 5–10 additional working days. Colour chips can be provided before the production order is confirmed.

QC checkpoints and salt-spray targets

Buyers can request a salt-spray hour target with the RFQ — typical export targets range from 500 hours (moderate inland) to 2000 hours (coastal or marine environments). Coating thickness reports and adhesion test results can be included in the proof pack before dispatch.

Export, MOQ & sample ordering

Export logistics for ornamental fence are different from panel-only fencing — panel protection and gate packing need separate planning.

Ornamental fence export requires more careful packing than standard chain link or welded mesh panels because the powder coat finish is more vulnerable to surface damage during ocean freight and container handling.

1. Sample order first

1–2 panels plus one post are shipped by express courier. Sample cost and shipping are quoted separately and credited against a full order. Use the sample to confirm finish quality, profile, colour, and height match.

2. Confirm finish route and colour

Lock the material (aluminum or steel), style, height, gate configuration, RAL colour, and salt-spray target. This locks the production specification before the proforma invoice is issued.

3. Packing plan and loading review

Panels are bundled with cardboard interleaving. Posts are bundled with protective wrapping. Gates are palletised with corner protection and stretch wrap. Loading plans and container utilisation estimates are shared before dispatch.

4. Documentation and dispatch

Commercial invoice, packing list, and bill of lading are prepared. Material certificates, coating thickness reports, and salt-spray test reports can be included if requested with the order confirmation. Sample panel orders ship in 3–5 working days; full production orders typically ship within 21–35 working days after PI confirmation.

One important reminder for ornamental fence export

Do not mix ornamental fence panels with heavy steel products in the same container without proper separation and padding. The weight and geometry of chain link rolls or steel posts can damage the powder coat finish of ornamental fence panels during ocean freight movement.
