Top buyer questions

These are the zinc steel fence questions buyers usually bring before the first RFQ.

What is zinc steel fence and how is it different from standard galvanized fence?

Zinc steel fence refers to a fence system where the steel frame and pickets are made from steel tubing or wire that has been zinc-coated — usually via hot-dip galvanizing or pre-galvanizing — before fabrication into panels. The zinc coating acts as a sacrificial anode: even if the surface is scratched, the zinc corrodes first rather than the steel underneath. Standard galvanized fence typically refers to fence components that are galvanized after welding, which can leave bare welds exposed. Zinc steel fence systems are designed so the zinc coating is applied at a stage that provides more complete coverage across the whole component, including cut ends and weld points, before assembly into panels and posts.

What heights and widths are available for zinc steel fence panels?

Common export heights range from 600 mm to 2400 mm, with 900 mm, 1200 mm, 1500 mm, 1800 mm, and 2100 mm being the most frequently requested sizes. Panel widths are typically 2400 mm or 3000 mm for standard production, though custom widths can be supported for project-specific orders. Height selection depends on the application: lower heights suit garden and pool fencing; mid-range heights fit residential perimeter and school boundaries; taller heights are used for commercial, industrial, and municipal projects requiring greater perimeter security. When requesting a quote, specify the height, panel width, number of rails, and whether the project is for a market with specific standards such as AS, BS / EN, or ASTM-style requirements.

What top styles are available: flat-top, spear-top, or decorative?

Three main top-style categories cover most residential and commercial perimeter requirements:

  • Flat-top: Clean horizontal profile with no exposed points. Often chosen for modern residential communities, commercial frontage, and projects where a lower-profile look is preferred. Easier to install privacy screening on top.
  • Spear-top: Pointed tops on each picket or at panel intervals. The most common ornamental style for residential villas, schools, and municipal boundaries where a traditional decorative look is expected. Provides a slight anti-climb deterrent.
  • Decorative / privacy: Curved, punched, or laser-cut panel tops that add visual distinction. Used for upscale residential, commercial, and projects where appearance is a significant buyer requirement. Typically commands a higher price point and longer lead time for custom punching or laser work.

Style selection should reference the destination market and project type: spear-top is dominant in UK, Australia, and European residential markets; flat-top is more common in North American modern residential and commercial projects.

What finish options are available: galvanized, powder coat, PVC, or duplex?

Four finish routes are typically offered, with selection driven by destination climate, budget, and aesthetic requirement:

  • Hot-dip galvanized only: Best for industrial, utility, and cost-focused projects. Zinc coating weight is typically 70–275 g/m². Sufficient for most inland and mild-climate applications. Not recommended as a final visible finish in high-humidity or coastal environments without overcoat.
  • Pre-galvanized + powder coat: Steel is zinc-coated before fabrication, then powder-coated over the zinc layer after welding. Produces a clean, colored finish. Coating thickness typically 60–120 microns. Good for residential, commercial, and decorative perimeter projects. The powder coat layer is the primary color; the zinc layer beneath provides corrosion resistance.
  • Hot-dip galvanized + powder coat (duplex): Full hot-dip galvanizing after fabrication, then powder coat over the zinc. Best corrosion protection available in this product family. Suitable for coastal, high-humidity, and high-salinity environments. Higher cost but longer maintenance-free life. Standard RAL colors available; custom colors supported for larger orders.
  • PVC / PE coated: PVC or polyethylene coating applied over galvanized wire or tube. Typically used for pool fencing, garden fence, and applications where a smooth, tactile surface and color options are valued. Thickness 0.4–1.0 mm. Good chemical and UV resistance. Not recommended in areas with significant abrasion risk.
Does zinc steel fence work in coastal areas, and which finish should I choose?

Zinc steel fence is used in coastal environments, but finish selection is critical. Salt-laden air accelerates corrosion on any steel surface. For coastal applications:

  • Duplex coating (hot-dip galvanized + powder coat) is the recommended route. The hot-dip galvanizing provides cathodic protection at cut edges and weld points; the powder coat layer seals the surface and provides the visible color finish.
  • Minimum galvanized zinc weight: 275 g/m² hot-dip galvanizing for coastal use, with powder coat top-coat. Do not use pre-galvanized-only finish in a coastal environment.
  • PVC-coated systems also perform well in coastal pool and garden applications, provided the PVC coating is undamaged and the substrate underneath is properly galvanized.
  • Avoid: unpowder-coated hot-dip galvanized only in coastal areas. The bright zinc surface will oxidize (white rust) quickly in salt air and the appearance will deteriorate within one season without maintenance.

For project buyers in Australia, UK, New Zealand, and Canada coastal zones, confirm the finish route and zinc weight in writing before the PO. This is one of the most common points of post-delivery dispute in zinc steel fence export orders.

What accessories are included in a complete zinc steel fence system?

A complete zinc steel fence system quote should include more than just panels. The accessories that matter most:

  • Posts: Square, rectangular, or decorative profile posts matching the panel style. Typically 60 × 60 mm, 80 × 80 mm, or custom. Post height = fence height + 600–750 mm buried or surface-mounted depth.
  • Post caps: Metal or plastic caps that seal post tops and prevent water ingress. Essential for maintaining corrosion resistance on hollow posts.
  • Brackets and fixings: Panel-to-post brackets, screws, bolts, and anti-theft bolt options. Stainless steel fixings are recommended for coastal projects.
  • Rail clips or U-channels: Used to attach horizontal rails to posts or panels.
  • Gates: Pedestrian gates (900–1200 mm width) and vehicle gates (single swing 1200–3000 mm; double swing; or sliding). Gate hardware includes hinges, latch, drop bolt, and optional automation hardware.
  • Privacy slats: Optional HDPE or metal slats inserted into the fence panel for privacy, dust control, or wind load reduction.

Specify the full accessory scope in the first RFQ message so the quotation covers the complete system rather than panels alone.

What is the minimum order quantity, and do you support custom sizes?

MOQ: Standard MOQ is typically 50 panels per size/height for production orders. Lower quantities can be supported for sample and trial orders at a higher unit cost. Distributors and repeat-order buyers should confirm the MOQ structure for their specific size combination.

Custom sizes: Yes, custom heights, panel widths, rail counts, picket spacing, and mesh openings are supported. The manufacturing process uses tooling that can accommodate custom dimensions within the production capability. For custom sizes, expect 15–25% higher unit cost compared to standard sizes and 2–4 additional weeks of production lead time. Custom RAL colors typically require a minimum of 200 panels per color for powder coat runs. Provide drawings or sketches with dimensions in the first inquiry for an accurate custom quotation.

Can I get samples, finish swatches, and spec sheets before placing a full order?

Yes. Sample and documentation support is available for serious buyers before the first PO:

  • Sample panels: 1–2 panels per size/finish combination, typically charged at the production cost of the panel plus shipping. Sample panels are the most reliable way to verify finish quality, welding consistency, and dimensional accuracy.
  • Finish swatches: RAL color cards and powder coat chip sets can be mailed separately for color selection without a full panel sample.
  • Specification sheets: Panel drawings, post specifications, gate schedules, and accessory lists are prepared as part of the quotation process and shared as PDF files for buyer review and internal approval.
  • Sample + spec pack: Request a combined sample and drawing pack that includes one panel, one post, the relevant bracket set, and the full specification sheet for your target market. This is the most common format used by importers and distributors to run internal qualification before a full container order.

  • Common heights: 900–2100 mm
  • Best markets: North America, Australia commercial, urban developments

  • Common heights: 1200–1800 mm (residential), 2000–2400 mm (commercial)

  • Panel width: 2400 / 3000 mm (standard); custom supported
  • Rail count: 2 rails (up to 1200 mm), 3 rails (1200–1800 mm), 4 rails (1800+ mm)

Custom & export questions

Custom sizing, OEM, export packing, and MOQ — the logistics questions that affect the first order most.

Export buyers who ask these questions early in the inquiry process get more accurate quotes and fewer surprises at the shipping stage.

Can zinc steel fence be OEM-branded or packaged with custom labels?

Yes. OEM support for zinc steel fence includes custom logo labeling on posts and panels, custom color box or pallet labeling, specification sheets with buyer branding, and data sheet customization. Minimum order requirements vary by customization type: logo labeling typically requires 200+ panels per SKU; custom packaging and labeling typically requires a dedicated production run of 500+ panels. Custom RAL color matching requires a minimum of 200 panels per color for powder coat runs. Provide brand guidelines, label artwork, and order quantity in the first inquiry so the OEM feasibility and cost impact can be confirmed before quotation.

How is zinc steel fence packed for export — what are the container loading options?

Export packing for zinc steel fence typically follows one of three approaches depending on order size and destination:

  • Bundle packing (standard): Panels stacked, strapped, and wrapped in waterproof film. Posts bundled separately with end caps installed. Gates crated or bundled. Cost-effective for full-container orders over 200 panels.
  • Pallet packing: Panels placed on wooden pallets (typically 6–10 panels per pallet), wrapped and strapped. Posts stacked on separate pallets. Recommended for port-to-port delivery where forklift unloading is available.
  • Stillage / cage packing: Returnable steel stillages used for repeat-order buyers and rental fleet supply. Reduces panel damage in transit and speeds up unloading. Higher upfront cost but lower per-trip cost for recurring orders.

Mixed-container loading is supported: panels, posts, gates, and accessories can be loaded together in one 20 GP or 40 HQ container. Provide the full accessory list in the RFQ so loading efficiency can be calculated before the container is confirmed.

What lead time should I expect for standard and custom zinc steel fence orders?

Lead times depend on order size, finish route, and current factory schedule:

  • Standard sizes, powder coat or galvanized: 15–25 production days from order confirmation
  • Standard sizes, duplex (hot-dip + powder coat): 20–30 production days
  • Custom sizes or custom RAL colors: 25–40 production days
  • Sample orders: 5–10 working days (panels only); 10–15 working days (full sample kits with posts and accessories)
  • Shipping: 25–45 days to major overseas ports depending on destination and shipping method (LCL, 20 GP, 40 GP, or 40 HQ)

Request the production slot confirmation before the order is placed, particularly for projects with fixed installation deadlines. The most common delay cause is not production — it is waiting for color confirmation or drawing approval from the buyer's side.

How long does zinc steel fence last, and what maintenance does it need?

Lifespan depends on finish route and environment:

  • Hot-dip galvanized only: 15–25 years in inland environments; 5–10 years in coastal environments without maintenance
  • Pre-galv + powder coat: 20–30 years inland; 10–15 years mild coastal; requires periodic inspection of coating integrity
  • Duplex (hot-dip + powder coat): 30–50 years inland; 15–25 years coastal; best maintenance profile in the zinc steel family
  • PVC coated: 15–25 years; UV degradation of PVC is the primary lifespan driver in sunny climates

Maintenance is minimal for all finish routes if the initial finish specification was correct for the environment. Touch-up paint for minor scratches is recommended within the first 12 months on powder-coated installations. Inspect fixings and gate hardware annually.
