Production process & durability proof

Buyers trust zinc steel fence suppliers more when the production flow and durability language are visible before the quote.

The strongest ornamental and zinc steel export pages combine a readable production walkthrough with a plain-language durability and warranty note. That signals the factory controls the process, not just the product.

01 - Tube cutting & preparation

Tube stock is cut to length, deburred, and checked for straightness before picket and rail profiles are formed. Wall thickness and tube dimensions are logged against the purchase spec.

02 - Welding & assembly

Pickets are welded to rails by machine or semi-auto TIG/MIG. Weld quality is reviewed for penetration, spatter, and consistency. Panel flatness is checked against tolerance before moving to coating.

03 - Pre-treatment & coating

Panels go through surface cleaning, iron phosphate or zinc phosphate pre-treatment, then the selected finish route: hot-dip galvanizing, pre-galvanized + powder coat, or duplex (galvanized + powder coat over).

04 - Salt-spray & adhesion checks

Finish samples can be tested in a salt-spray chamber per ASTM B117 or ISO 9227 to verify coating integrity before the production run ships. Buyers who need test reports can request them with the RFQ.

05 - Counting, labeling & packing

Panels, posts, gates, and hardware are counted, bundled, protected at edges, labeled by bundle or panel number, and palletized for container or breakbulk loading.

06 - Dispatch & documentation

Export documentation includes packing list, commercial invoice, and optionally COO or form EUR1. Container loading photos or videos can be shared before dispatch on request.

Durability & warranty note

Coating performance depends on the environment and finish route selected. Hot-dip galvanized finish is the baseline for most export markets. Powder coat over galvanized adds color retention. Buyers can request a salt-spray hour target with the RFQ; typical export targets range from 500 to 2000 hours depending on the market and project specification.

Gate styles & accessories system

Gate scope is one of the most common missing items in zinc steel fence RFQs - surface it early.

Buyers who only quote fence panels still need to budget for gates. This block makes the gate range visible as a system component so it gets included in the first inquiry, not discovered after the first quote.

Pedestrian gate - single leaf

Typical opening of 900-1200 mm. Frame matches the fence panel profile; infill matches or complements the selected picket or rail style. Available in flat-top and spear-top variants.

  • Image slot: pedestrian gate front view
  • Standard hinge and latch set included

Vehicle gate - double leaf or single swing

Typical opening of 3000-6000 mm for double-leaf, or 3000-5000 mm for single swing. Frame reinforcement and drop bolt or ground latch are standard. Vehicle gates can be automated with motor preparation on request.

  • Image slot: vehicle gate front view
  • Motor preparation available for automated gate projects

Sliding gate - cantilever or track

For sites where swing clearance is limited. Cantilever sliding gates are standard for most export applications. Track sliding gates are an option for sites with consistent ground level.

  • Image slot: sliding gate front view
  • Cantilever gate requires post span of gate width x 1.5

FAQ

Questions zinc steel fence buyers usually ask before the first order.

Is zinc steel fence the same as steel picket fence or ornamental steel fence?

They overlap in buying intent. Some buyers search by material logic, some by appearance. This page is built to bridge zinc steel, steel picket, and ornamental-style demand while keeping the offer positioned as a complete export-ready fence system.

Can you supply posts, gates, and accessories together with the fence panels?

Yes. Buyers can request a full system quotation covering panels, posts, gates, brackets, caps, bolts, and spare fittings so the shipment is closer to the actual installation scope.

How do I choose between flat-top, spear-top, and decorative privacy styles?

Start from the use case. Flat-top works well for modern community and school projects, spear-top fits more traditional ornamental demand, and decorative privacy routes suit appearance-led residential or commercial frontage applications.

Do you support samples or trial orders for new zinc steel fence buyers?

Yes. Sample planning, finish review, and trial orders can be discussed first when the buyer needs to confirm appearance, coating, or system fit before a larger purchase.

Can the shipment include packing and loading requirements for easier unloading?

Yes. Buyers can mention pallet logic, labeling, gate separation, accessory counting, and other packing expectations in the first RFQ so the shipment is easier to receive on arrival.

Can I send a hand sketch, line layout, or gate schedule instead of a finished drawing?

Yes. A simple layout sketch, gate schedule, or marked-up site photo is often enough to move from broad product interest to a more itemized zinc steel fence quotation.

What information makes the first quote more accurate?
