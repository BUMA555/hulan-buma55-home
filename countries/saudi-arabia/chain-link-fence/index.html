Finish selection for Saudi climate zones

Choosing the right coating is the single most important decision for chain link longevity in Saudi Arabia.

Saudi Arabia has two distinct corrosion pressure zones. Getting the finish wrong is the most common cause of early chain link fence failure in KSA projects — and the most common complaint in post-delivery disputes.

Inland desert zones — Riyadh, Najd, Al-Qassim

Hot, dry, low-humidity conditions. Standard hot-dip galvanizing (zinc coating weight minimum 60 g/m², typically 120 g/m² for heavier commercial grade) provides 15–20 years of service life without additional coating.

  • PVC coating is optional for inland desert — adds cost without proportional corrosion benefit in low-humidity environments
  • Thicker wire diameter (3.0 mm+) recommended for construction site durability

Coastal and Red Sea zones — Jeddah, Jubail, Yanbu, Ras Tanura

High humidity, salt air, and airborne chloride. Hot-dip galvanizing alone will degrade faster than inland. PVC-coated chain link over hot-dip galvanized wire is the recommended minimum for these zones.

  • PVC colour options: black, green, white, grey (RAL custom available for larger orders)
  • PVC adhesion to galvanized wire must be certified via salt spray testing (TÜV, SGS, or equivalent)
  • Expected service life: 20–25+ years with PVC over HDG in coastal Saudi environments

Industrial and petrochemical zones — Jubail, Ras Tanura, Yanbu

Chemical airborne pollution (H₂S, CO₂, chlorides) in addition to salt air. Aramco and SABIC projects typically require dual-layer protection and documentation packages that include MTC, chemical composition reports, and surface treatment certification.

  • Confirm finish requirements against project specification before ordering
  • Aramco-style documentation available: MTC, ISO 9001 cert, test reports, material data sheets
Finish selection quick reference for Saudi buyers
  • Riyadh / inland desert → Hot-dip galvanized, 3.0 mm+ wire, 50×50 mm mesh
  • Jeddah / Red Sea coast → PVC-coated over HDG, 3.0 mm+ wire, black or green
  • Jubail / Ras Tanura / industrial → PVC over HDG, minimum 3.5 mm, Aramco documentation package
  • Construction site temporary → HDG, 2.5–3.0 mm, knuckled edge, 10 m rolls
  • Government / municipality → Confirm SASO compliance requirement and consultant spec

Frequently asked questions — Saudi Arabia chain link fence

The eight questions Saudi buyers ask before placing a chain link fence order.

What is the minimum order quantity for chain link fence to Saudi Arabia?

MOQ for chain link fence fabric is typically 50 rolls (10 m rolls = 500 m minimum fabric). Full system packages (fabric + posts + rails + fittings) start at quantities that fill one 20ft container — approximately 1,000–1,500 m depending on height and roll length. LCL (partial container) is available for trial orders starting at approximately 200–300 m, though unit costs are higher. Use the RFQ form to describe your project scale and we will confirm the most cost-effective quantity and shipping route.

What finish is recommended for Jeddah coastal installations?

For Jeddah, Yanbu, Jubail, and other coastal Saudi locations, PVC-coated chain link fence over hot-dip galvanized wire is the recommended minimum. Salt air accelerates corrosion on standard hot-dip galvanized surfaces. PVC over HDG provides a dual-layer protection system: the galvanized layer provides sacrificial anode protection, and the PVC coating provides a physical barrier against chloride-laden air. Black and green are the standard colour options; RAL custom colours are available for orders of 500+ rolls. Expected service life in Jeddah coastal conditions: 20–25+ years with PVC over HDG.

Do you have experience supplying Aramco and SABIC-affiliated projects?

Yes. We have supplied chain link fence and perimeter systems to contractors and project buyers operating within the Aramco supply chain, including Jubail industrial zone projects and Ras Tanura facility installations. We provide the full Aramco-style documentation package: Mill Test Certificate (MTC) with chemical composition and zinc coating weight, ISO 9001 quality certificate, material data sheets, and pre-shipment inspection reports. For first-time Aramco contractors, we can walk through the documentation requirements against your project purchase order before confirming the order.

Can you provide SASO-compliant documentation for Saudi municipality projects?

Yes. For projects that require SASO (Saudi Standards, Quality and Metrology Organization) compliance or a named municipal or government consultant specification, we produce a SASO-aligned documentation package including Certificate of Origin, MTC, compliance declaration, and product data sheets. The key step is confirming the exact specification reference in the project tender or BOQ before quoting, so the documentation package is built to match the stated requirement. Send us the specification reference and we will confirm documentation scope and any implications for product specification.

What is the lead time from order confirmation to shipment?

For chain link fence fabric rolls in standard galvanized finish, lead time is 10–15 working days for quantities up to 5,000 rolls. PVC-coated rolls require 15–20 working days due to the additional coating process. Full system packages (fabric + posts + rails + fittings) for one 40ft container load typically require 15–20 working days. Sea freight from Shanghai or Qingdao to Jeddah is 18–22 days; to Dammam is 20–25 days. Total door-to-port delivery time: approximately 5–7 weeks from order confirmation for standard KSA orders. Urgent orders can be coordinated on a case-by-case basis.

What are the payment terms for first-time Saudi buyers?

Standard payment terms for first-time buyers are 30% deposit by T/T, 70% balance against copy of Bill of Lading (B/L). For repeat orders and orders above $20,000, we can discuss open account terms (O/A 30–60 days) subject to credit approval. For larger project orders ($50,000+), we can discuss split shipment payment schedules aligned with project phase deliveries. All payment terms are confirmed in the Proforma Invoice (PI) before order confirmation. Letter of Credit (L/C) is available for confirmed orders above $30,000 from approved banks.

Can you ship to Riyadh with inland transport included?

Yes. We coordinate inland transport from Jeddah or Dammam port to Riyadh and other inland destinations through our freight forwarder network. The typical route is: port of arrival (Jeddah or Dammam) → Saudi customs clearance → inland transport to Riyadh, Dammam, Jubail, or other KSA city. Freight forwarder coordination is handled as part of the export service. For DDP (Delivered Duty Paid) incoterms, inland transport and Saudi customs clearance are included in the quoted price. For CFR or CIF quotes, inland transport is arranged separately at the buyer's request.

What gate options are available with the chain link fence system?

Gate options include single pedestrian gates (0.9 m–1.2 m width), double vehicle gates (3.0 m–6.0 m width), and sliding gates for larger industrial access points. Gates are available with chain link infill to match the fence fabric specification (same mesh, wire diameter, and finish as the fence line). Gate frames are typically made from galvanized steel tube, with hinge hardware, lock plate, and latch included. Custom gate sizes can be manufactured for project-specific access requirements. Use the RFQ form to specify gate quantities, widths, and any access control hardware requirements.
