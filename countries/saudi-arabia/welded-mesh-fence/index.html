KSA Climate Fit

Finish routing for Saudi Arabia environments

KSA presents three distinct corrosion challenges: coastal salt air (Jeddah, Yanbu, Jubail), high UV and sand abrasion (Riyadh, Dammam), and industrial atmospheric pollution. Route the finish to match the site environment.

🌊

Coastal — Jeddah / Yanbu / Jubail

Dual-layer hot-dip galvanizing (≥ 230 g/m² zinc) + polyester powder coat (≥ 80 µm) is the minimum recommended specification for salt-air zones. RAL 6005 green or RAL 7016 anthracite hides dust and sand accumulation well.

☀️

Inland — Riyadh / Dammam / Kharj

Single-layer hot-dip galvanizing handles inland conditions adequately. Adding powder coat extends coating life by protecting the zinc layer from UV degradation and sand abrasion. Light colours (RAL 9010 white) reflect heat and reduce surface temperature.

🏭

Industrial — Jubail / Ras Tanura

Industrial chemical atmospheres require the full dual-layer treatment. For petrochemical sites, specify zinc-aluminum alloy (ZAM) coating or increased zinc coating weight (≥ 350 g/m²) on the galvanizing layer. We can advise based on the specific site report.

📦

Construction Site — Temporary Use

For temporary construction hoardings (typical rental yard scenario), hot-dip galvanized only is the most cost-effective route. Panels are reusable across multiple projects when the galvanized layer remains intact. Replace or re-galvanize when zinc is consumed.

Design and Specification Tips

  • For perimeter fencing adjacent to paved roads, specify in-ground posts with concrete anchor rather than surface-mounted bases to prevent vehicle displacement
  • For desert environments with wind-blown sand, V-beam (profiled) mesh panels provide additional rigidity without increasing wire gauge
  • If anti-climb properties are required, 358 mesh panels are the correct specification — do not substitute standard welded mesh and expect equivalent security
  • For projects requiring SABER or SASO certification (Saudi Standards compliance), notify us at the RFQ stage — documentation and testing can be arranged
  • Mixed-container loads (welded mesh + posts + gates + accessories) can be optimized to reduce per-unit shipping cost for orders above 2× 40' HQ
FAQ

Frequently asked questions — Saudi Arabia welded mesh fence

What is the difference between welded mesh and 358 security fence?

Welded mesh panels have larger apertures (typically 50×50 mm or 50×100 mm) and are designed for deterrence and boundary marking. 358 mesh has a 76.2×12.7 mm aperture — small enough to prevent finger grip (anti-climb) and resist cutting with hand tools. If the application needs anti-climb and anti-cut performance, specify 358 mesh. For standard perimeter, construction site, or utility boundary use, welded mesh is the more economical choice.

What finish is best for Saudi Arabia coastal environments?

For Jeddah, Yanbu, and Jubail coastal zones, specify hot-dip galvanizing (zinc coating ≥ 230 g/m² per ISO 1461) plus polyester powder coat (≥ 80 µm). This dual-layer treatment protects against both salt air corrosion and UV degradation. RAL 6005 green and RAL 7016 anthracite are the most common colour choices for industrial environments in KSA.

Can you supply a full perimeter system with posts, gates, and accessories?

Yes. We supply complete welded mesh perimeter systems including panels, posts (SHS or C-section), panel clamps or clips, tension bars, gates (swing or sliding, manual or automated), and topping options (razor wire coil or concertina tape). One order, one shipment, one source for the full system.
