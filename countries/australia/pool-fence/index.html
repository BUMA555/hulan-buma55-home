Australia pool fence FAQ

Common questions from Australia-focused pool barrier buyers.

Do you supply pool fence systems for Australia with AS 1926.1-related planning in mind?

Yes. This page is written around the barrier logic Australia buyers usually reference: height, opening, spacing, and gate-closing direction associated with AS 1926.1-style project notes. Send your actual certifier or project wording in the RFQ so the quote follows the right path.

What material is more common for Australia pool fence — aluminum or steel?

Aluminum is often the first choice for low maintenance and clean residential presentation. Steel can still be a good route where buyers want stronger structure, value-focused supply, or visual consistency with adjacent steel fencing and gates.

Can you quote gates, hinges, and latches together with the fence?

Yes. Pool barrier projects should normally be quoted as a system: panels, posts, gates, hinges, self-latching hardware, brackets, caps, and fixings. Quoting the gate scope separately too late is one of the main causes of project delay.

What colours are most common for Australia pool fence?

Black is still the most common route, followed by white and dark grey. If the project needs a custom powder-coat or a Monument-style dark finish, include that note in the first RFQ so the quote reflects the real finish path.

Can one order include pool fence plus tubular or other residential products?

Yes. Mixed residential containers can make sense for distributors, developers, and builder programs that need pool fence, tubular steel, gates, or other perimeter products together. Put the full scope into one RFQ for better packing and pricing logic.

What should I include in the first Australia pool fence inquiry?

Use this format: destination city or port + project type + barrier note + material route + height and quantity + gate scope + colour / finish + lead time target. That gives enough context for a more useful first quote.
