Why 358 Fence for Australian High-Security Projects

358 anti-climb fence — named for its 3" × 0.5" × 8-gauge mesh — is the benchmark product for perimeter security in Australia's correctional, defence, and critical infrastructure sectors. The tight 76.2 × 12.7 mm aperture prevents fingers and toes from gaining purchase, making climbing or cutting the panel with standard tools extremely difficult.

  • Standard anti-climb aperture: 76.2 mm (H) × 12.7 mm (W)
  • Wire diameter: 4.0 mm (standard) or 5.0 mm (heavy duty)
  • Panel heights: 1.8 m, 2.0 m, 2.4 m, 2.7 m, 3.0 m, 3.6 m
  • Panel width: 2,500 mm standard (custom widths available)
  • Finish: hot-dip galvanised (HDG), powder-coated (RAL), or HDG + powder-coat duplex
  • Post options: square hollow section (SHS), round hollow section (RHS), or H-post

Typical Australian Applications

  • Correctional facilities: perimeter security for prisons, remand centres, and juvenile detention
  • Defence installations: base perimeters, ammo stores, and restricted-access zones
  • Critical infrastructure: substations, water treatment plants, communications towers, and pipelines
  • Airport and port security: airside perimeter, cargo areas, and customs zones
  • Commercial high-risk sites: data centres, pharmaceutical warehouses, and logistics hubs
  • Schools and government buildings: as a deterrent anti-intrusion layer combined with CCTV

Finish and Colour Options

All panels are available in hot-dip galvanised (HDG) as the baseline corrosion protection, with optional powder-coat in standard RAL colours:

RAL 6005 – Moss Green RAL 7016 – Anthracite Grey RAL 9005 – Jet Black RAL 6009 – Fir Green RAL 9010 – Pure White Custom RAL on request

Duplex finish (HDG + powder coat) is recommended for coastal Queensland, NT, and WA sites with high salt-air exposure.
