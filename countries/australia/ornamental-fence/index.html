Australia ornamental FAQ

Common questions from Australia tubular steel and ornamental fence buyers.

What is the difference between ornamental fence, tubular steel fence, and steel picket fence in Australia?

In practical RFQs, these terms often overlap. Tubular steel and steel picket usually describe the panel construction route, while ornamental fence describes the appearance-led category. The right quote depends on style, height, gate scope, finish, and application.

Do you supply complete tubular fence systems or just panels?

We can supply the full system: panels, posts, gates, hinges, latches, brackets, caps, fixings, and export packing support. Complete system quoting usually saves time and reduces mismatch on site.

What colours do Australia buyers usually ask for?

Black remains common, while Monument-style dark grey, Basalt-style dark grey, and Woodland Grey-style finishes are also frequent discussion points. If you have a project colour or powder-coat code, include it in the first RFQ.

Can ornamental fence be used for schools and civic sites in Australia?

Yes. Tubular and ornamental systems are often chosen where the site wants visibility and a cleaner public-facing appearance. Final suitability depends on the project requirement, gate logic, and expected durability.

Can one Australia order include ornamental fence plus pool fence or temporary fence?

Yes. Mixed-category orders are common for distributors and larger builders. Put all required fence families, gate needs, and accessories into one RFQ so the quote and packing plan match the real scope.

Do Australian buyers usually ask for samples first?

Often yes, especially for colour confirmation, gate hardware review, or when the buyer is validating the panel style before a larger program. Mention whether you need a sample panel, finish chip, or drawing support.

When should I use the Australia pool fence page instead of this page?

If the project starts from AS 1926.1 barrier logic, self-closing gate hardware, or pool-specific compliance-sensitive questions, use the Australia Pool Fence page. This ornamental page is better for front fence, estate, school, and civic routes.

What is the best first-message format for an Australia ornamental RFQ?

Use this format: city or port + application + style route + height and quantity + finish + gate and hardware scope + target lead time. That gives enough context to move straight into a useful quote.
