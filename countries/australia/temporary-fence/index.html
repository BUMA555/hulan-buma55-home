Frequently asked questions

FAQs for Australian Temporary Fence Buyers

Are your temporary fence panels certified to AS 4687:2022?

Our panels are manufactured to the dimensional requirements and material specifications referenced in AS 4687:2022 ?particularly Sections 3 (Classification), 4 (Dimensional requirements) and 5 (Performance requirements). We are not independently certified by a Australian-accredited laboratory, but we provide Mill Test Certificates and factory test reports covering wire diameter, mesh aperture, frame OD/wall thickness and zinc coating weight. These documents support your local certifier's sign-off process. We recommend engaging a Australian engineer to review our documentation package for your specific project permit requirements.

What is the standard temporary fence panel size used in Australia?

2400 × 2100 mm (width × height) is the dominant size used across Australian hire fleets and construction sites. This is the size we manufacture and export as our primary SKU. We also offer 2400 × 1800 mm (compact/event) and 2400 × 2400 mm (high-security / wind-exposed) as alternatives.

Can I mix rubber feet and concrete feet in the same order?

Yes. Many buyers order a mix ?rubber block feet for standard panels on paved surfaces, and concrete block feet for panels on the perimeter or exposed corners. Specify quantities for each type in your RFQ and we will allocate accordingly in the container loading plan.

Do you supply privacy mesh and shade cloth?

Yes. HDPE knitted shade/privacy mesh is available in three coverage densities: 50% (partial shade), 70% (standard privacy), 90% (high privacy / wind protection). Standard colours: green, black, orange. Specify coverage percentage and colour in your RFQ. Mesh is supplied in rolls or pre-cut panels with cable-tie attachment holes.

What are your minimum order quantities (MOQ) for Australia?
