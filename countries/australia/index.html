Australia market FAQ

Do your temporary fence panels align with AS 4687 expectations?

Our Australia temporary fence route is written around AS 4687-style buying language, including standard panel-size expectations, anti-climb mesh logic, and complete accessory scope. For project-led orders, include any tender wording or local requirement in the RFQ so the offer is shaped correctly.

What ports do you usually quote for Australia?

Melbourne, Sydney, Brisbane, and Fremantle are the most common port directions surfaced in Australia RFQs. If the delivery is inland or Adelaide-linked, include the final destination so the shipping logic is clearer from the first round.

Can one Australia order include temporary fence, welded mesh, and residential steel together?

Yes. Mixed-category orders are often the best way for distributors and larger project buyers to build a stronger landed-cost program. Put all required fence families, gate needs, and accessories into one RFQ so the packing and commercial route match the real scope.

What is the most common Australia temporary fence panel size?

The most common Australia market reference is 2100 mm high × 2400 mm wide, but project-specific sizes and configurations are also possible. Accessory scope matters just as much as panel size, so list feet, clamps, stays, gates, and any privacy add-ons in the inquiry.

Do you support anti-climb and high-security projects for Australia?

Yes. 358 anti-climb fence is the clearest route for higher-security projects such as utilities, schools, restricted sites, and infrastructure-related perimeter. If there are topping, gate-control, or specification notes, include them in the first message.

Can you quote pool or barrier-related projects too?

Do Australian buyers usually ask for samples first?

What is the best first-message format for an Australia fence RFQ?

Use this format: city or port + application + fence route + size and quantity + finish + posts / gates / accessories + standard note + target lead time. That gives enough context to move straight into a useful quote instead of a generic reply.
