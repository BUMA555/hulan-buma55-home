Why 358 for the UK market

The fence system that UK security buyers specify by name.

358 mesh is the most recognised high-security perimeter product in the UK public and private sectors. Its aperture geometry, wire gauge, and finish options map directly to LPS 1175, Secured by Design, and CPNI-aligned procurement routes.

76.2×12.7 mm Anti-climb aperture

The tight mesh opening prevents finger- and toe-holds, defeating scaling without the need for additional topping on many specifications.

4.0 / 5.0 mm Wire diameter options

4.0 mm standard meets most commercial and institutional requirements. 5.0 mm heavy-duty is available for MOD, prison, and utility-rated systems.

HDG + PC UK weather-rated finish

Hot-dip galvanised base coat (min. 60 µm per BS EN ISO 1461) plus powder coat duplex provides long-term corrosion resistance for British coastal and inland environments.

1.8–4.0 m Height range

Full height range covers lower-security site perimeters through to Category B/C prison specification and MOD high-security compound requirements.

Standards & compliance notes

UK procurement moves faster when standards are raised upfront.

The most common delay in UK 358 fence quotations is missing or late clarification on security ratings, finish routes, and documentation requirements. Raising these points in the initial RFQ allows us to return a complete, spec-matched quote on the first response.

LPS 1175 — Loss Prevention Certification

System-rated options for public-sector security tenders.

LPS 1175 covers physical security performance of fence systems from A1 (low risk) to B3 (high risk). If your project references an LPS rating, include it in the RFQ — we can confirm system compatibility or suggest the nearest equivalent.

  • Specify: A1, A2, B1, B2, or B3 required
  • Include third-party test report requirement
  • Note if SCI (Security Construction & Equipment) listing is needed
Secured by Design (SBD)

Police-approved specification route for UK developments.

Secured by Design is the official police initiative for crime prevention through environmental design. Many UK local authorities, housing associations, and institutional buyers require SBD compliance. We can confirm 358 compatibility with SBD-accredited systems and provide supporting documentation.

  • Specify SBD compliance requirement in RFQ
  • Confirm panel geometry, wire gauge, and post system match SBD requirements
  • Available: LPCB / BRE test data and SBD letter of compliance from manufacturer
BS EN Standards — Mesh & Coating

Documentation route for commercial and public-sector buyers.

BS EN 10223-7 covers welded mesh fence panels. BS EN ISO 1461 covers hot-dip galvanising. Both are routinely requested in UK tender submissions. Mill certificates and EN test reports are supplied as standard with each order.

  • BS EN 10223-7: wire mesh panel geometry and tolerances
  • BS EN ISO 1461: HDG coating mass and uniformity
  • BS 1722-10: chain link and welded mesh fence — relevant for some perimeter specs

Common questions

FAQ — UK 358 fence procurement.

What is the minimum order for 358 mesh panels?

There is no fixed minimum order quantity for standard panel sizes. We handle pilot orders from 10 panels through to full-container and multi-container project volumes. Smaller orders are typically packed LCL.

Can 358 fence be supplied with Secured by Design certification?

358 mesh panels can be confirmed against SBD-accredited system requirements. We can provide supporting manufacturer documentation and, where required, direct buyers to the relevant LPCB / BRE product listings.

What heights are typically specified for UK schools?

UK schools most commonly specify 2.0 m or 2.4 m 358 mesh with HDG + powder coat finish. Some specify 3.0 m for sixth-form and college sites. Secured by Design notes are frequently included in school procurement.

Can panels be powder coated in a custom RAL colour?

Yes. Standard stock colours are RAL 6005, 7016, and 9005. Any other RAL colour is available on request with a colour-matching lead time of 5–7 working days.

What is the difference between 358 mesh and heavy-duty welded mesh?

358 mesh has a 76.2×12.7 mm aperture — small enough to prevent finger-holds — making it anti-climb by geometry. Heavy-duty welded mesh typically has larger apertures (50×50 mm, 75×150 mm) and is anti-cut but not anti-climb without topping. The choice depends on the threat assessment and specification requirement.

Do you supply gates with 358 fence packages?

Yes. We supply swing gates, double-leaf gates, and motorised sliding gates to match 358 mesh panel specifications. Gate schedules — including opening width, leaf configuration, and hardware — should be included in the RFQ.
