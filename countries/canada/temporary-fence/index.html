Canadian Market Notes

  • CSA vs AS 4687: Canada does not have a federal temporary fence standard equivalent to Australia's AS 4687, but municipal and provincial specs often call for minimum 3.0mm infill wire and hot-dip galvanizing — both standard in our supply.
  • Rubber vs concrete base: Rubber block bases dominate Canadian rental fleets (lighter, less pavement damage). We supply both; most Canada orders specify rubber.
  • Winter storage: Panels are typically stacked outdoors. Hot-dip galvanizing handles freeze-thaw cycling without surface failure.
  • Import duty: Most temporary fence panels enter Canada at 0% MFN duty under HS 7314.xx. Confirm with your broker for project-specific classification.
  • Lead time: 25–35 days production + transit. Container booking from Tianjin or Qingdao to Vancouver/Montreal typically 18–22 days.
