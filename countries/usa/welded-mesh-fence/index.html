Standards & compliance for US projects

ASTM references and documentation provided with every export shipment.

US procurement teams and specifiers need documentation before they can approve a foreign supplier. We provide ASTM reference documentation and compliance certificates so submittals move faster.

ASTM A392 — Chain Link Fence Fabric

Welded wire mesh used as fence fabric references ASTM A392 for wire quality, tensile strength, and zinc coating weight. Mill certs to this standard are included in the submittal pack.

ASTM A1065 / A500 — Structural Tube

Square, peach, and round posts are produced from cold-formed welded structural tubing. Mill certs to ASTM A500 (structural tubing) are provided per post shipment.

ASTM A123 — Hot-Dip Galvanizing

Hot-dip galvanizing certificates to ASTM A123 confirm zinc coating weight (typically 460 g/m² minimum) for panels, posts, and hardware before dispatch.

BS 1722-12 — Steel Fence Standard

BS 1722-12 (welded mesh fence) is commonly referenced by US specifiers for welded wire perimeter fence. We align product dimensions and performance to this standard.

Coating Thickness & Adhesion Reports

Powder coat and duplex coating thickness reports (in mils or µm) and adhesion test results are available on request and are included in formal project submittals.

Pre-Shipment Inspection (SGS / BV)

SGS or Bureau Veritas inspection before loading is available on request for orders over $20,000. Inspection report is provided before the Bill of Lading is issued.

USA Welded Mesh FAQ

Questions US buyers usually ask before requesting a welded mesh quote.

What is the difference between 3D V-bend, flat panel, and twin wire welded mesh?

3D V-bend panels have a pressed V-curve at the top and bottom rails for rigidity and a cleaner appearance — most common in US commercial and school applications. Flat panels are simpler and used for budget or utility applications. Twin wire (868/656) panels have two horizontal wires sandwiching vertical wires for higher impact resistance — preferred for industrial yards, logistics parks, and higher-security sites.

Do your welded mesh panels meet ASTM standards for US projects?

Yes. We reference ASTM A392 for wire fence fabric, ASTM A500 for structural posts, and ASTM A123 for hot-dip galvanizing. Mill Test Certificates and Certificates of Conformity are provided with every shipment. For formal project submittals, send the project specification and we will confirm compliance before quoting.

Can I get powder-coated welded mesh fence in a specific RAL color?

Yes. Standard RAL colors are RAL 6005 (green), RAL 7016 (anthracite grey), and RAL 9005 (black). Custom RAL colors are available for project orders — specify the RAL code in the RFQ. Duplex coating (HDG + powder coat) is standard for projects requiring both corrosion resistance and colour consistency.

What is the minimum order quantity for a trial USA order?

A 20 ft container trial order is the most common starting point for first-time USA buyers — typically 150–200 panels plus the matching post, bracket, and gate complement. LCL consolidation can accommodate smaller orders. For distributor stock programs, mixed containers and repeat-order scheduling are available.

Are gates supplied with matching welded mesh panels?

Yes. Gates are quoted as part of the full fence system. Pedestrian gates (single swing, 1.0 m clear opening) and vehicle gates (double swing, 3.0–6.0 m clear opening) are available with frames matching the panel mesh and finish. Gate hardware (hinges, latch, drop bolt, ground latch) is included in the gate kit price.

What documentation is provided for US Customs entry?

Every shipment includes: Commercial Invoice, Packing List, Bill of Lading, Certificate of Origin, Mill Test Certificates, and Certificate of Conformity. Fumigation certificates are included where applicable. SGS or BV pre-shipment inspection is available on request for orders over $20,000.
