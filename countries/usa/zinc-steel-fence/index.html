Fence styles for the USA market

Match the fence style to the HOA requirement and project character before quoting.

US residential steel fence is sold by style profile as much as by dimension. The profile determines the visual character of the completed fence line and is usually specified in the HOA guidelines or architectural standards before the buyer requests a quote.

Flat-Top Residential Steel Fence

The cleanest, most contemporary residential profile — a horizontal rail system with vertical pickets finished flat across the top. Most common in modern HOA communities and contemporary residential developments.

  • Best for: modern HOA communities, contemporary residential, commercial frontage
  • Heights: 3 ft, 4 ft, 5 ft, 6 ft (most common: 4 ft and 5 ft)
  • Rails: 2-rail standard for ≤4 ft; 3-rail for 5–6 ft
  • Match with: flat-top pedestrian and driveway gates

Spear-Top Residential Steel Fence

Classic pointed-picket profile with a rolled or pointed top — the most widely recognized residential steel fence style in US neighborhoods. Provides a traditional appearance while maintaining the strength of steel construction.

  • Best for: traditional HOA communities, residential subdivisions, townhome complexes
  • Heights: 3 ft, 4 ft, 5 ft, 6 ft
  • Pickets: pointed spear, forged spear, ball-cap spear variants
  • Match with: spear-top pedestrian and driveway gates

Gothic / Arch-Top Residential Steel Fence

Curved-top profile with gothic arch or scalloped rail geometry — used in upscale residential, historical district, and architectural preservation projects where style compliance requires a more ornamental profile.

  • Best for: historic district projects, upscale residential, golf communities
  • Custom radii and arch heights available for project-specific compliance
  • Gate must match arch profile — quote together with fence line

Privacy Panel (Closed-Picket) Steel Fence

Tighter picket spacing with no vertical gaps — used where noise reduction, wind screening, or visual barrier is required alongside the perimeter function. Not ICC-compliant in all jurisdictions — check local building codes.

  • Best for: commercial perimeters, pool enclosures, noise-sensitive sites
  • Available as 2-rail or 3-rail with flush or tongue-and-groove picket arrangement
  • Verify with local building department before specifying

Industrial / Commercial Ornamental Steel

Heavier-gauge steel with wider picket spacing and larger rail sections — used for commercial properties, industrial office parks, and municipal facilities where durability drives the specification over residential aesthetics.

  • Best for: commercial office parks, municipal buildings, industrial campuses
  • Heavier gauge steel (typ. 16 ga pickets vs. 18–20 ga residential)
  • Available with anti-ram or crash-rated base options for security applications

Standards & compliance documentation

ASTM and ICC reference standards provided with every USA residential steel fence order.

US building departments, HOA boards, and procurement officers need documented standards before approving a foreign supplier. We provide the reference standards, test data, and specification sheets that US specifiers typically require.

ASTM F2408 — Ornamental Fence Systems

Standard Specification for Ornamental Fence Systems Used as Security Perimeters for highways, parks, schools, and commercial properties. This is the primary ASTM reference for residential and commercial ornamental steel fence in the USA.

ASTM F2659 — Preliminary Pitch

Standard Specification for Steel and Aluminum Fence used for temporary and permanent perimeter enclosure. Referenced for material quality and dimensional consistency.

ICC IBC Chapter 32 — Fences

International Building Code Chapter 32 covers fence height limitations, permit requirements, and location rules. We provide spec sheets and drawing notes that reference IBC Chapter 32 for municipal permit applications.

BS EN 13438 — Powder Coating

European standard for powder coating on steel and aluminum — referenced by US specifiers for coating quality, adhesion, and durability testing. Coating thickness reports to this standard are provided on request.

ICC-ES Report (when available)

ICC Evaluation Service reports provide third-party verification that a product meets US building code requirements. We can discuss ICC-ES report availability for specific product lines — mention this in the RFQ if your project requires it.

USA Residential Steel Fence FAQ

Questions US buyers usually ask before requesting a residential steel fence quote.

Do you supply ICC-compliant documentation for US building permit submittals?

Yes. We provide ASTM F2408 reference specifications, elevation drawings, post spacing notes, and finish documentation packages. For projects requiring ICC Evaluation Service reports, mention this in the RFQ and we will confirm availability and lead time for the specific product line.

What is the difference between your steel fence and aluminum fence for US projects?

Steel fence with hot-dip galvanized + powder coat duplex offers higher impact resistance and is preferred for commercial, industrial, and high-traffic residential applications. Aluminum fence is lighter and naturally rust-free — preferred for pool enclosures and coastal applications where weight or corrosion resistance is the primary concern. We supply both; the choice depends on your project requirements, climate zone, and budget.

Can you match a specific HOA color or architectural color standard?

Yes. Standard RAL colors are RAL 6005 (green), RAL 7016 (anthracite grey), RAL 9005 (black), and RAL 7012 (grey). Custom RAL colors are available for project orders — send the HOA design standard color code or architectural specification with the RFQ and we confirm the matching powder coat and lead time.

What is the minimum order quantity for a US residential steel fence trial order?

A 20 ft container is the typical starting point for a US residential steel fence trial — covering approximately 150–250 linear feet of fence plus the matching posts and gate kits. For smaller project quantities, LCL consolidation or mixed-container programs are available. Sample panel sets ship via DHL/FedEx within 5 business days.

How do you handle gate hardware for pool barrier compliance?

We supply gate kits with self-closing hinges, self-latching latch hardware, and spring-loaded closing mechanisms as standard for pool-enclosure applications. For projects requiring ADA-compliant gate hardware or specific local pool code requirements, send the applicable code section with the RFQ and we confirm the hardware specification before quoting.

What is the typical lead time from RFQ to delivery at a US port?

For standard styles and colors: 15–25 days production + 18–35 days ocean transit depending on US port. For custom RAL colors or non-standard profiles: add 5–10 days to production. We provide a detailed production and shipping schedule with the quotation so you can plan the jobsite delivery window before committing to the order.
