Why twin wire mesh fence for the USA market

Twin wire mesh fence is the security-perimeter standard for US commercial, industrial, and institutional projects.

Twin wire mesh fence has become the preferred perimeter fence specification for US commercial and industrial buyers who need more rigidity and cut-resistance than standard welded mesh, but better visibility and lower cost than 358 security fence. The double horizontal wire design resists bolt cutters and hand saws while maintaining an open mesh appearance compatible with CCTV surveillance and patrol observation — two key requirements for modern US security perimeter specifications.

868 and 656 wire configurations for US security levels

The 868 configuration (6mm top / 8mm vertical / 6mm bottom) is the most common US specification for commercial and industrial security perimeters — providing high rigidity and cut resistance with standard hand tools. The 656 configuration (5/6/5mm) is used for standard perimeter and residential applications where cost optimization is important. Both are hot-dip galvanized and powder coated in our standard duplex finish for US outdoor environments.

Duplex finish engineered for US climate zones

Hot-dip galvanizing (275g/m2 zinc coating minimum) followed by electrostatic polyester powder coating gives twin wire mesh fence a duplex finish rated for 20+ years in US coastal, industrial, and inland climate zones. The powder coat layer in RAL 6005 (green), RAL 9005 (black), or custom RAL adds UV resistance and aesthetic quality for municipal, commercial, and residential applications.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions for USA twin wire mesh fence buyers.

What is the difference between 656 and 868 twin wire mesh?

The numbers refer to wire diameters in millimeters: 656 means 5mm top horizontal / 6mm vertical / 5mm bottom horizontal. 868 means 6mm top / 8mm vertical / 6mm bottom. The 868 configuration is significantly more rigid and cut-resistant — the standard choice for US commercial and industrial security applications. 656 is used for standard perimeter, residential, and cost-optimized applications where security requirements are moderate. If you are unsure, describe the application and we will recommend the right configuration.

What is the difference between twin wire mesh and 358 security fence?

358 security fence has a much smaller mesh opening — typically 12.5x75mm or 12.5x100mm — which makes it essentially impossible to climb or cut with hand tools. Twin wire mesh has a larger opening — typically 50x200mm or 75x200mm — which provides better visibility for CCTV and patrol observation. Twin wire mesh is less expensive than 358 for most commercial and industrial applications and is the preferred choice where visibility is important alongside security. 358 is specified for prisons, military installations, and maximum-security zones.

What mesh opening should I choose?

50x200mm (2" x 8") is the standard mesh opening for most US twin wire mesh applications — providing a good balance between cut resistance, structural rigidity, and visibility. 75x200mm (3" x 8") provides slightly better visibility and airflow and is used where aesthetics and surveillance are priorities. 100x200mm is cost-optimized for lower-security applications. We will recommend the right mesh opening based on your application security requirements.
