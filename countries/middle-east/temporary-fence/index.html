Why GCC Buyers Choose Temporary Fencing

Temporary fence panels are the first-choice perimeter solution for construction sites, mega-project corridors, public events, and road works across the UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Kuwait, Bahrain, and Oman. The reusable, tool-free design suits the GCC's fast-moving project environment: panels go up in hours, can be relocated as site phases shift, and comply with municipal safety and crowd-control requirements in major cities including Dubai, Abu Dhabi, Riyadh, and Doha.

  • No ground anchoring required — concrete-filled base feet for hard desert surfaces
  • Hot-dip galvanised finish survives Gulf UV, sand abrasion, and salt-laden coastal air
  • Panel mesh prevents unauthorised site access while maintaining contractor visibility
  • Rapid deployment suits NEOM, Vision 2030, and Expo-type project timelines
  • Fully reusable — panels last 5–10 years with minimal maintenance

GCC Application Breakdown

  • Construction perimeters: Standard 3m panels with rubber base feet are the norm for UAE and Saudi project sites; concrete-filled bases preferred for Riyadh and Jeddah sandy ground conditions
  • Road works and utility corridors: 2.0m height with anti-lift clamps required under Abu Dhabi and Dubai municipality road-works standards
  • Events and crowd control: Lightweight 32mm frame panels with quick-release clamps for F1 Abu Dhabi, Saudi Cup, Qatar sports events
  • Port and logistics compounds: Heavy-duty panels at Jebel Ali Free Zone, King Abdullah Port, and RAKIA for cargo security perimeters
  • NEOM and giga-projects: Large-volume panel supply with on-call replenishment for phased construction corridors

Accessories Supplied

  • Rubber base feet (standard, fits 32mm and 40mm frame tube)
  • Concrete-filled steel base feet (for loose desert sand and gravel)
  • Steel sleeve clamps (galvanised, 2-bolt, fits 32mm / 40mm)
  • Anti-lift clamps with security pin
  • Bracing stays (diagonal, 1.2m, galvanised tube)

  • Reflective safety strips (required on Dubai and Abu Dhabi road-adjacent sites)
