Frequently Asked Questions — Welded Mesh Fence for South Africa

What mesh size is best for South African mining perimeter applications?

For South African mining perimeters, we recommend 50×100mm mesh with 4.0mm wire diameter and 3D V-bend profile. This combination provides the rigidity required for high-traffic mine access roads, anti-climb deterrence at heights up to 2.4m, and sufficient visibility for surveillance. For lower-security agricultural boundaries, 75×150mm or 100×200mm mesh with 3.0–3.5mm wire offers a cost-effective solution.

Can your welded mesh panels be used for residential estates in South Africa?

Yes. PVC-coated welded mesh panels (HDG + RAL 6005 green or RAL 7016 grey) are a popular choice for South African residential estates in Gauteng, Western Cape, and KwaZulu-Natal. The PVC coating provides an aesthetically clean finish while the HDG substrate ensures long-term corrosion resistance. We recommend 2.0–2.4m panel heights with 50×100mm or 60×150mm mesh for estate perimeter applications.

Do you provide SABS compliance documentation for project bids?

Yes. We provide a SABS compliance documentation package that includes material test reports (MTR), weld quality statements, coating specification sheets, and a factory compliance declaration. This package is suitable for submission with municipal and government project bids in South Africa. The documentation package is included for orders ≥200m; for smaller orders, it is available for a nominal admin fee. Always request documentation during your RFQ.

What is the minimum order quantity for welded mesh fence panels?

MOQ is generally 100 panels for standard specifications. For mixed container orders or project orders, we can accommodate smaller trial quantities. For custom specifications (non-standard mesh size, wire diameter, or height), the MOQ increases to 200 panels. Trial orders and sample panels are available — contact us to discuss your specific requirements.

What is the difference between hot-dip galvanized and PVC-coated welded mesh?

Hot-dip galvanizing (HDG) applies a zinc coating of 60–80 g/m² to the steel wire before welding, providing strong corrosion resistance for 15–20 years in most environments. PVC coating (applied over HDG) adds a 0.4–0.6mm plastic layer in any RAL colour, providing additional corrosion protection and a cleaner aesthetic. For South Africa's diverse climate — from coastal Durban humidity to inland Limpopo dryness — HDG + PVC (double-layer) is the best choice for coastal and high-humidity areas; HDG alone is sufficient for most inland applications.

How many panels fit in a 40HQ container?

A 40HQ can load approximately 800–1,200 flat welded mesh panels depending on panel dimensions, wire gauge, and mesh size. For 1.8m height panels at 4.0mm wire with 50×100mm mesh, approximately 800–900 panels fit in a 40HQ. Taller panels or heavier wire gauges reduce the count. Posts, clamps, and gates are bundled separately and loaded in the same container. We provide a precise loading plan with your order confirmation.

You can also order panels only if you have existing posts. Custom gate configurations (single leaf, double leaf, sliding) are available. All components use the same surface treatment as the panels.
