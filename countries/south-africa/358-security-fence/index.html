Frequently asked questions

Questions South African buyers ask before ordering 358 security fence.

What is the minimum order quantity for 358 security fence to South Africa?

MOQ is 20 panels for standard stock orders. For custom sizes, finishes, or heights, MOQ starts at 50 panels per specification. Full-container orders (20 ft or 40 ft) offer the most competitive per-unit pricing. We include a detailed packing and container loading plan for orders from 1 x 20 ft container upwards.

What is the typical lead time from order confirmation to shipment?

Standard panel orders (HDG finish) ship within 10-15 business days from order confirmation and deposit. Heavy-duty specifications (5.0 mm wire, custom heights above 3.0 m) and powder coat finishes require 15-20 business days. We confirm the production schedule and estimated ship date in writing before production begins.

What 358 wire diameter should I specify for a South African mining perimeter?

For standard industrial or commercial perimeters, 4.0 mm wire is sufficient. For mining operations, correctional facilities, or high-threat environments, specify 5.0 mm heavy-duty wire. Anti-cut performance improves significantly with the heavier gauge, and the additional cost per linear metre is typically 15-20% above standard.

What panel height is most common for South African projects?

For industrial estates and commercial perimeters: 2.0 m - 2.7 m is most common. For mining perimeters: 2.4 m - 3.0 m with razor coil topping. For correctional and high-security facilities: 3.0 m - 3.6 m minimum, often with V-arm brackets and electric fence integration. We can advise on height vs. topping combinations based on your threat assessment.

Can SABS compliance documentation be provided for mining board or government tenders?

Yes. We prepare SABS compliance documentation packages for project bid submissions including mill certs, material test reports, zinc coating weight certificates, and dimensional inspection reports. Include the requirement, project type, and tender deadline in your initial RFQ - we prepare the documentation alongside the commercial pricing so both arrive together. Typical turnaround is 3-5 business days.

What finish is best for South African coastal environments - Durban, Cape Town, Port Elizabeth?

For sites within 5 km of the coast (Durban, Richards Bay, Port Elizabeth, Cape Town Atlantic seaboard), we recommend HDG + thermosetting powder coat (marine-grade, RAL 6005 or RAL 7016). The duplex system provides both the sacrificial protection of HDG and the barrier protection of powder coat - critical for salt air environments. HDG alone is acceptable for inland projects in Gauteng, Limpopo, and the Highveld.

What topping options are available for 358 fence in South Africa?

Standard options: razor coil (single strand concertina, double strand concertina), flat wrap razor mesh, V-arm brackets with or without razor coil, and electric fence integration rail. The V-arm + razor coil combination is most common for SA mining and correctional applications. Electric fence integration requires advance specification - we work with your electric fence contractor to ensure bracket compatibility.

Can you supply gates that match 358 mesh infill panels?

Yes. Gates are supplied with 358 mesh infill matching the fence panels - same wire diameter, same aperture, same finish. Gate options include single swing (1.0 m - 2.0 m), double swing (3.0 m - 6.0 m), and motorised sliding gates (up to 12.0 m wide). Automation prep (chain rack, control housing, safety beam brackets) is available - specify at RFQ stage.

What payment terms do you offer for South African buyers?

Standard payment terms for first-time South African buyers: 30% deposit by T/T, balance against shipping documents (B/L copy). For repeat orders or established accounts, we can discuss open account terms (NET 30) subject to credit approval. Irrevocable Letter of Credit (LC) available for orders above USD 50,000. We accept T/T and L/C; invoices in USD.

Can sample panels be sent to South Africa before a bulk order?

Yes. Sample panels (1-2 panels, 1.8 m or 2.0 m height) and accessory samples (post section, fixings) can be sent by air freight to Johannesburg or Durban. Typical cost for 2 panels + accessories via DHL or air freight is USD 150-300 depending on weight. Sample cost is credited against subsequent bulk orders above a defined threshold. Request samples in your RFQ - we send a sample pack recommendation based on your project type.
